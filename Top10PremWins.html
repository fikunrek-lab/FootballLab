Top 10 Arsenal Premier League Wins of the 2025/26 Season

From derby dominance to last-minute winners, these were the wins that shaped Arsenal’s historic 2025/26 Premier League campaign.

Honourable Mention – Arsenal 2–1 Crystal Palace (Away)

Goalscorers: Gabriel Jesus, Noni Madueke

Assists: Gabriel Martinelli, Kai Havertz

Context: After ending a 22-year wait for the Premier League title, Arsenal arrived at Selhurst Park for their crowning ceremony. It was a celebration of a historic achievement. The Gunners secured a 2–1 victory before lifting the Premier League trophy, with every member of the squad cementing their place in Arsenal history.

#10 – Arsenal 3–0 Fulham (Home)

Goalscorers: Viktor Gyökeres (2), Bukayo Saka

Assists: Viktor Gyökeres, Leandro Trossard

Man of the Match: Viktor Gyökeres

Context: In the eight games that preceded this match, Arsenal had a record of two wins, two draws, and four losses. Heading into the match, there were serious questions about Arsenal's attack and whether it had enough firepower to keep pace with Manchester City. Those doubts quickly disappeared. After just 45 minutes, Arsenal were already 3–0 up.

#9 – Arsenal 5–0 Leeds United (Home)

Goalscorers: Jurrien Timber (2), Viktor Gyökeres (2), Bukayo Saka

Assists: Jurrien Timber, Riccardo Calafiori (2), Declan Rice

Man of the Match: Jurrien Timber

Context: Arsenal's first home game of the 2025/26 season also marked Viktor Gyökeres' home debut. The Gunners eased all nerves with a dominant 5–0 victory, while the Swedish striker announced himself to the Emirates crowd with a brace.

#8 – Arsenal 2–0 Everton (Home)

Goalscorers: Viktor Gyökeres, Max Dowman

Assists: Piero Hincapie, Gabriel Martinelli

Man of the Match: David Raya

Context: After keeping only one clean sheet in their previous five matches, Arsenal battled their way to a gritty 2–0 victory over Everton. The match will be remembered forever for Max Dowman, who became the youngest goalscorer in Premier League history at just 16 years old.

#7 – Arsenal 1–0 Manchester United (Away)

Goalscorers: Riccardo Calafiori

Assists: Declan Rice

Man of the Match: David Raya

Context: Arsenal's opening match of the season provided the first opportunity to prove themselves as genuine title contenders. A disciplined and professional display against one of their fiercest rivals secured a valuable 1–0 victory.

#6 – Arsenal 4–1 Aston Villa (Home)

Goalscorers: Gabriel Magalhães, Martin Zubimendi, Leandro Trossard, Gabriel Jesus

Assists: Jurrien Timber, Martin Ødegaard, Bukayo Saka, Leandro Trossard

Man of the Match: Leandro Trossard

Context: After losing to Aston Villa less than two weeks earlier, Arsenal responded emphatically. The 4–1 victory in the rematch announced them as serious contenders and showed signs that this squad was finally ready to go the distance.

#5 – Arsenal 4–1 Tottenham Hotspur (Away)

Goalscorers: Viktor Gyökeres (2), Eberechi Eze (2)

Assists: Jurrien Timber, Martin Ødegaard

Man of the Match: Leandro Trossard

Context: After drawing with bottom-of-the-table Wolves, Arsenal found themselves in a difficult position. With only two wins from their previous seven league matches, they needed a statement performance. Eze and Gyökeres both scored braces as Arsenal cruised to a 4–1 North London Derby victory and completed an 8–2 aggregate scoreline against Spurs for the season. What do we think of Tottenham?

#4 – Arsenal 2–1 Newcastle United (Away)

Goalscorers: Mikel Merino, Gabriel Magalhães

Assists: Declan Rice, Martin Ødegaard

Man of the Match: Gabriel Magalhães

Context: Arsenal started the season off strong, but a trip to St James' Park is never straightforward. The Gunners left it late, with Gabriel Magalhães scoring a dramatic 96th-minute winner to take home all three points.

#3 – Arsenal 4–1 Tottenham Hotspur (Home)

Goalscorers: Eberechi Eze (3), Leandro Trossard

Assists: Mikel Merino, Declan Rice, Leandro Trossard, Jurrien Timber

Man of the Match: Eberechi Eze

Context: Unbeaten in their previous seven Premier League matches, Arsenal hosted Spurs for the 201st North London Derby. Eberechi Eze delivered one of the greatest debut derby performances in history, becoming only the fourth player to score a hat-trick in the North London Derby and the first player ever to score a hat-trick on his North London Derby debut.

#2 – Arsenal 1–0 West Ham United (Away)

Goalscorers: Leandro Trossard

Assists: Martin Ødegaard

Man of the Match: David Raya

Context: Following a painful defeat to Manchester City two weeks earlier, Arsenal responded with professionalism and resilience. The hard-fought 1–0 victory put the Gunners back in control of the title race and left them just one win away from ending their 22-year wait for a Premier League title.

#1 – Arsenal 1–0 Burnley (Home)

Goalscorers: Kai Havertz

Assists: Bukayo Saka

Man of the Match: Kai Havertz

Context: Arsenal entered Matchday 37 with the chance to move five points clear of Manchester City and place enormous pressure on the defending champions. In the 37th minute, Kai Havertz met a Bukayo Saka corner to score the only goal of the game. The match ended 1-0. The rest is history.
